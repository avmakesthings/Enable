Approximately 3.6 million people over the age of 15 in the US require a wheelchair to assist with mobility

En-Able is a cross-platform, open source application that aims to provide the most comprehensive universally accessible trip planning and routing for urban areas.

The larger aim of this project is to develop a scalable platform that can be used to facilitate mobility in many other cities in the future.

The initial release of En-Able will be based in San Francisco. We are looking for San Francisco based organizations and users who think they could benefit from this app to collaborate with us on this project.

Our app features

Trip planning and routing

Our advanced routing algorithm takes into account key accessibility datasets and heuristics from prior trips to suggest the best possible way to get from A to B.

Realtime public transit updates

Recognizing that the accessibility of trains and buses can soometimes vary based on infrastructure breakdowns or peak loads, we factor in realtime updates on the status of public transit.

User aggregated data

We recognize that the only way to create a truly comprehensive accessibility dataset is by giving users the opportunity to provide ratings on routes and to flag obstacles, and to incorporate this information into our routing.

Social networking

We hope to cultivate a vibrant community of active users and to achieve integration with major social networks, making enable much more than a mapping and routing application.
